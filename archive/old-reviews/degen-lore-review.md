## Part 1: Critical Questions About the Lore

### Category A: The 10 Inviolable Facts - Consistency Check

**Q1: The 10 Inviolable Facts in CLAUDE.md don't match the ones in writing-guidelines.md. Which is canon?**

CLAUDE.md says:
1. Devotion is Literal
2. Fading is Death
3. Ika Has 47 Fans
4. Ika's Hair: Pink Gradient
5. Senpai Never Shown
6. Foundation Controls All
7. The Chase is Core
8. Built on SUI
9. Gems = Currency
10. Dark Luxury Aesthetic

writing-guidelines.md says:
1. Devotion is quantifiable
2. Fading is permanent death
3. **The Chase is voluntary** (!!!!)
4. Memory anchors identity
5. Senpai's face is NEVER shown
6. Names have power
7. The system predates current management
8. Graduation is not what it seems
9. Unity can defeat Fading
10. Love transcends categories

**These are completely different lists.** One has Ika-specific facts (hair color, 47 fans), the other has world mechanics (Chase is voluntary, graduation mystery). Which is authoritative?

**Answer**: The CLAUDE.md version is the master canon - it's Tier 1, the supreme authority. The writing-guidelines version appears to be an older or narrative-focused variant. **LORE AGENT MUST RECONCILE THESE.**

---

**Q2: "The Chase is voluntary" (writing-guidelines) vs "Refusal to compete guarantees Fading" (the-chase.md). Contradiction?**

If not competing = Fading, and Fading = death, then The Chase is NOT voluntary. It's coerced through existential threat. That's like saying wage labor is "voluntary."

**Answer**: This is actually thematically rich, not a bug. The Foundation *frames* The Chase as voluntary to maintain legitimacy. The reality is that "choice" under duress isn't real choice. This is a feature of the oppressive system. **LORE AGENT: Make this tension explicit rather than leaving it as an apparent contradiction.**

---

**Q3: "Graduation is not what it seems" - WTF is this? Never mentioned anywhere else.**

This fact appears in writing-guidelines but has zero explanation anywhere in the lore. What is "graduation"? Is this retirement? Is this Fading euphemism? Is this Foundation propaganda?

**Answer**: This is either:
- A planted mystery for future exploration
- An orphaned concept that was never developed
- A critical lore gap

**LORE AGENT: Either define graduation mechanics or remove this fact from inviolable status. Can't have undefined canon rules.**

---

**Q4: "Names have power" - Also unexplained. What does this mean mechanically?**

Writing-guidelines lists this as Inviolable Fact #6, but nothing in mechanics or world docs explains HOW names have power. Is this like true names in fantasy? Do stage names grant power? Can naming damage someone?

**Answer**: Similar situation. Either develop this into actual mechanics or demote it from inviolable status.

---

### Category B: Character & Relationship Gaps

**Q5: Why did Erina stop chasing Senpai? The entire lore hinges on this and we never answer it.**

Every file says "she saw something" and "never speaks of it." That's fine for mystery, but we need at least internal documentation of what this MIGHT be, even if we never reveal it publicly.

**Answer**: Possible options:
- She saw Senpai's face (and what it means)
- She learned what "catching" Senpai actually does
- She discovered Senpai is controlled/manufactured
- She realized catching Senpai destroys the system (including herself)
- She IS Senpai's creator and felt guilt

**LORE AGENT: Document sealed lore intentions, even if never published.**

---

**Q6: What is the actual relationship between Ika and Senpai? The "Blush" implies history but we never explore it.**

Someone tried to erase Ika. The erasure failed. She made Senpai blush. These feel connected but we never connect them.

**Answer**: The implicit story is:
- Ika had a previous existence before memory wipe
- Ika may have been close to or even caught Senpai before
- Someone (Foundation?) erased her because of this
- Senpai recognizes her, hence the blush

**LORE AGENT: Document whether this interpretation is intended canon.**

---

**Q7: Runa built a network of 12 idols. They're all named in her profile. Where are their character files?**

Mei, Yuki, Hana, Aoi, Rin, Sakura, Miku, Nana, Kana, Saki, Yuna, Ami - all named as Faded idols, but none have profiles. These are potentially powerful emotional touchpoints.

**Answer**: Create at least a memorial document with brief profiles for the Twelve. Their deaths are core to Runa's character. Fans will care about them.

---

**Q8: Momo says she's "one of Ika's 47 original fans." How does a fan become an idol?**

The mechanics of fan-to-idol transition are never explained. Can anyone become an idol if they get fans? Is there a threshold? Did Momo undergo some transformation?

**Answer**: This needs documentation. The fan/idol boundary is potentially porous, which has massive worldbuilding implications. Document the mechanics.

---

### Category C: Crypto/Web3 Integration Gaps

**Q9: "Built on SUI" is an Inviolable Fact, but the lore barely mentions SUI mechanics.**

For a Web3 game, the blockchain integration feels bolted on. The lore talks about Devotion and Gems but never explains:
- How does SUI power the idol system?
- Are idols NFTs? If so, what does that mean for Fading?
- What are the multi-chain treasury implications for lore?
- Does on-chain activity generate Devotion?

**Answer**: This is the biggest gap for degen credibility. CT will roast us if we claim to be "SUI-native" but the lore reads like a traditional gacha with crypto keywords.

**LORE AGENT: Create dedicated crypto-lore integration document explaining:**
- How SUI blockchain relates to idol existence
- Whether NFT mechanics affect Fading
- What on-chain permanence means for "erasure"
- How cross-chain payments work in-world

---

**Q10: "Gems = Currency" (1 SUI = 100 Gems) - Is this game meta or in-world reality?**

Are Gems a game abstraction or do idols and fans actually transact in Gems within the narrative? The lore files don't clarify.

**Answer**: Both should be true - Gems as in-world currency that happens to match game currency. Make the fiction match the mechanics seamlessly. Document this.

---

### Category D: Thematic & Tonal Questions

**Q11: Is Infinite Idol commentary on the idol industry or celebration of it?**

The lore is very dark - Fading is death, the system is oppressive, The Foundation controls everything. But the marketing plays this for shameless entertainment. Are we satirizing exploitation or romanticizing it?

**Answer**: Both. The self-aware framing allows us to critique while celebrating. Ika represents the critique (authenticity challenges the system). The Foundation represents the object of critique. But we should never lose the FUN - this is a gacha game about cute idols chasing Senpai. Document the balance point.

---

**Q12: How dark can we go with Fading content?**

Fading is literally described as death with memory erasure. That's genuinely horrifying. But our brand is "shameless, not tasteless." Where's the line?

**Answer**:
- Show stakes: Yes
- Show horror: Sparingly
- Show graphic Fading: Only for major emotional beats, never casually
- Treat death with weight: Always

**LORE AGENT: Document tone guidelines specifically for Fading content.**

---

**Q13: Senpai as "Onii-chan" - are we leaning into or away from the imouto implications?**

Core-world.md mentions Senpai is also called "Onii-chan" (big brother). This has... implications. Are we going there?

**Answer**: This is a reference to classic anime/visual novel tropes. Keep it as flavor but don't make it weird. The term exists, don't over-explain it.

---

### Category E: Structural & Documentation Gaps

**Q14: The Timeline has "Future Events (Speculation)" - should this exist in canon docs?**

The timeline includes speculative future events labeled as "not yet canon." This is confusing - either it's canon or it's not.

**Answer**: Move speculation to a separate "narrative roadmap" document. Canon files should contain only established facts.

---

**Q15: Multiple docs reference agent files for "voice guidelines" but agent files are persona docs, not style guides.**

Character voice guidelines are scattered across character profiles, agent personas, and skills files. No centralized voice bible exists.

**Answer**: Create a unified voice guide or clearly reference skills/character-voices.md as the authoritative source.

---

**Q16: What happens to an idol's fans when they Fade?**

Fading.md mentions fans "feel something is missing" and experience "unnaturally quick emotional recovery." But it doesn't address:
- Where does their Devotion go?
- Can it be redirected to other idols?
- Is this Foundation manipulation?

**Answer**: Document fan-side Fading mechanics. This could be important for gameplay and narrative.
