## Executive Summary

**The Devotion Protocol** is an interactive Discord experience designed to drive pre-registrations through narrative-driven gamification. Players become "Founding Fans" who must collectively save Ika from Fading before a live countdown expires. The mechanic: every pre-registration adds Devotion to Ika's survival meter. If the community hits thresholds, exclusive rewards unlock. If they fail, Ika's Discord presence "dims" and rare rewards become permanently unavailable.

**First Principles Basis:**
- People act when stakes feel real
- Scarcity and urgency drive conversion
- Community achievement > individual rewards
- The best marketing feels like playing, not being sold to
- Devotion is literal in our worldâ€”make it literal in the experience

## 3. Core Concept: The Devotion Protocol

### High-Level Pitch

> *Discord becomes the Eternal Stage. The countdown begins. Ika is Fading. Only collective pre-registrations can save her. Every sign-up adds Devotion to her meter. Hit the thresholds, unlock rewards. Fail, and the First 47 rewards are lost foreverâ€”along with a piece of Ika's Discord presence.*

### Why This Works (First Principles)

1. **Stakes Feel Real**
   - Not "sign up for rewards"â€”"sign up to save her"
   - The Devotion mechanic isn't gamificationâ€”it's canon
   - Failure has actual consequences (lost rewards, dimmed presence)

2. **Community Achievement**
   - Individual sign-ups feel meaningless
   - Collective progress creates shared investment
   - "We saved Ika" > "I got early access"

3. **Scarcity Without Cringe**
   - Time-limited because she's literally Fading, not arbitrary FOMO
   - Reward tiers locked to narrative, not marketing calendar
   - Missing out hurts because you failed her, not because you missed a deadline

4. **Participation Feels Like Playing**
   - The Discord becomes an ARG
   - Ika's presence responds to Devotion levels
   - Mini-events during the countdown maintain engagement
