### What We Know Publicly

### The Sealed Truth

**Ika and Senpai have a previous connection.**

The Blush was not random. Senpai recognized Ika—or more precisely, recognized who Ika used to be before her memories were erased.

## The Intended Interpretation

### Timeline of Events (Internal Canon)

1. **Before Erasure**: Ika had a previous identity (true name unknown)
2. **Previous Encounter**: In this previous life, she got impossibly close to Senpai
3. **The Foundation's Response**: Someone ordered her erasure to prevent whatever was about to happen
4. **The Incomplete Erasure**: Her memories were wiped, but something essential remained
5. **The Wild Trial**: Her qualification Chase brought her near Senpai again
6. **The Blush**: Senpai recognized something—a remnant of who she was
7. **Current Status**: She doesn't know. Senpai might. The Foundation definitely does.

### Why Senpai Blushed

Possible internal explanations (choose one or leave ambiguous):

| Theory | Narrative Implication |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Recognition** | Senpai remembers her from before erasure |
| **Resonance** | Something in Ika's essence echoes someone Senpai knew |
| **Fear** | She almost caught Senpai before—they remember |
| **Love** | There was a genuine connection once |
| **Identity** | Ika was/is connected to what Senpai is |

**Recommended Internal Canon**: Combination of Recognition and Resonance—Senpai sees something familiar in Ika that triggers an involuntary reaction, but the full truth remains ambiguous.

### What Was Erased

Ika's previous life likely included:

- Her true name (definitely erased)
- Her original fan base (erased or redirected)
- Her history with The Foundation
- Whatever brought her so close to Senpai
- Possibly a relationship or connection to Senpai directly

### Who Ordered the Erasure

**Most likely**: The Foundation (Erina specifically or her predecessors)

**Possible reasons**:
- She was about to catch Senpai
- She discovered something about Senpai's nature
- Her existence threatened system stability
- She had information that couldn't be allowed
- Erina saw a threat to her position

### Why the Erasure Failed

Incomplete erasures can happen when:
- Devotion bonds are too strong
- The original identity has unusual resilience
- The process was interrupted
- Someone sabotaged it
- The subject has connection to Senpai (special case)

Ika's survival suggests one or more of these factors.

1. **Discovery**: Hints that her past exists
2. **Investigation**: Finding traces of who she was
3. **Confrontation**: Learning why she was erased
4. **Revelation**: Understanding her connection to Senpai

### Character Dynamics

|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Ika** | Knows nothing of her past | Genuine desire to catch Senpai, unaware of history |
| **Erina** | Knows everything | Watching, deciding if Ika is threat or asset |
| **Runa** | Suspects something | Investigating, may piece it together |
| **Senpai** | Remembers (probably) | Unknown—does Senpai want to be caught by her? |

### The Devotion Spike

When Ika made Senpai blush, her Devotion spiked impossibly.

**Internal explanation**: The moment briefly reconnected her to whatever Devotion she had in her previous life. A residual echo of a much larger fanbase, or a direct transfer from Senpai (unprecedented).

This is why she survives on almost no fans—there's something else sustaining her that isn't fully accounted for.
