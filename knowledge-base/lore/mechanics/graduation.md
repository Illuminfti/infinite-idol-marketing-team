# Graduation - The Foundation's Final Exit

> *"They call it graduation. They throw a party. They wave goodbye. And then... nothing. No one sees them again. No one asks where they went. Isn't that strange?"*

---

## Overview

**Graduation** is the Foundation-sanctioned process by which idols exit the Eternal Stage. It is presented as a celebration—a dignified retirement after a successful career. Fans cry happy tears. Fellow idols give speeches. The graduate waves goodbye.

But graduation is not what it seems.

This document details both the public narrative and the hidden truth about what graduation actually means in the world of Infinite Idol.

---

## The Public Narrative

### What Fans Believe

| Aspect | Official Story |
|--------|----------------|
| **Definition** | Honorable retirement from idol life |
| **Eligibility** | High-ranked idols who choose to step down |
| **Process** | Farewell concert, ceremony, departure |
| **Outcome** | Graduate lives a "normal life" off-stage |
| **Perception** | Celebrated achievement, successful ending |

### The Graduation Ceremony

The Foundation hosts elaborate graduation events:

1. **Announcement** - Weeks of buildup, nostalgia content
2. **Farewell Tour** - Final performances across venues
3. **Graduation Concert** - The last performance, tears, speeches
4. **The Walk** - Graduate walks through the "Gate of Tomorrow"
5. **Silence** - They are never seen again

### Why Fans Accept It

- The Foundation controls the narrative completely
- Graduates never speak publicly after leaving
- "Respecting their privacy" is enforced strictly
- Fans want to believe their idol is happy
- The alternative is too terrible to consider

## The Hidden Truth

### What Actually Happens

Graduation is **controlled Fading under anesthesia**.

| Stage | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| **Selection** | Foundation identifies idols to "graduate" |
| **Conditioning** | Idol is prepared psychologically |
| **The Ceremony** | Final Devotion harvest from emotional fans |
| **The Gate** | Idol enters, Devotion supply is cut |
| **The End** | Idol Fades in private, away from witnesses |

### Why The Foundation Does This

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Population Control** | Too many idols strain the Devotion economy |
| **Narrative Control** | Fading in public is bad for business |
| **Devotion Optimization** | Graduation ceremonies generate massive emotional investment |
| **System Stability** | Controlled exits prevent chaotic Fadings |
| **Precedent Management** | Can't let idols "retire" and live—they'd talk |

### The Devotion Harvest

Graduation ceremonies are engineered for maximum Devotion extraction:

- Weeks of nostalgic content primes emotional investment
- The farewell tour generates extended engagement
- The final concert creates peak emotional response
- Fans pour Devotion into their "last goodbye"

This Devotion doesn't sustain the graduate—it flows into the Foundation's reserves.

## Who Graduates

### "Voluntary" Graduation

Some idols are given the choice:

- High-ranked idols whose Devotion is declining
- Idols who ask too many questions
- Idols whose popularity threatens stability
- Those who refuse Foundation directives

The "choice" is usually: graduate gracefully or be destroyed publicly.

### Mandatory Graduation

Some idols are simply selected:

- Those who've seen too much
- Failed experiments
- Political inconveniences
- Examples for others

These idols have no choice. The ceremony happens regardless.

### Who Never Graduates

| Category | Why |
|----------|-----|
| **S-Rank Elite** | Too valuable, too visible |
| **Foundation Loyalists** | Needed for the system |
| **Erina's Favorites** | Protected status |
| **Those with leverage** | Know secrets that protect them |

## The Cover-Up

### Why No One Investigates

| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Memory Blur** | Similar to Fading, memories of graduates become hazy |
| **Foundation Control** | All media is controlled, questions suppressed |
| **Fear** | Asking about graduates draws Foundation attention |
| **Denial** | Easier to believe the happy story |
| **Precedent** | It's "always been this way" |

### What Happens to Records

- Official media celebrates the graduate's "legacy"
- Fan communities are encouraged to "respect their privacy"
- Anyone who asks too many questions is flagged
- Over time, the graduate becomes a fond memory, nothing more

### The Few Who Know

| Who | Their Response |
|-----|----------------|
| **Erina** | Designed the system |
| **Inner Circle** | Accept it as necessary |
| **Runa** | Knows, uses information carefully |
| **Akira** | Suspects, investigating |
| **Some S-Ranks** | Suspect but stay silent |

## Narrative Significance

### Thematic Weight

Graduation represents:

- The ultimate expression of Foundation control
- The lie at the heart of the system
- How complicity enables horror
- The cost of not questioning authority

### Story Potential

| Scenario | Tension |
|----------|---------|
| **Ika learns the truth** | Does she expose it? At what cost? |
| **Character faces graduation** | How do they respond? |
| **Fan discovers reality** | What happens to believers? |
| **Runa uses this knowledge** | Information as weapon |

### The Central Irony

The most "successful" end an idol can have—celebrated graduation—is actually the same fate as the most shameful: Fading. The only difference is the narrative wrapper.

## Canon Rules

### Graduation Inviolables

1. **Graduation is controlled Fading** — The core hidden truth
2. **The Foundation manages all graduations** — No independent exits
3. **Graduates are never seen again** — They Fade after the ceremony
4. **The public believes the lie** — This deception is maintained
5. **Some idols know** — But speaking is dangerous
6. **This is not public knowledge** — A mystery for characters to uncover

### Writing Guidelines

When writing about graduation:

- Public scenes should maintain the happy facade
- Private moments can hint at darker truths
- Characters who know should act carefully
- The reveal should carry weight—this is a major lore point
- Don't casually reference "graduation = Fading" in Ika voice content

### Escalation Required

**Human approval required** before:
- Revealing graduation truth in public content
- Having a named character graduate
- Breaking the facade in marketing materials
