# Voice and Tone - Infinite Idol Brand Guidelines

> *"We know exactly what we are. We're shameless about it. And we're damn good at it."*

---

## Overview

The Infinite Idol voice is **shameless but genuine, self-aware, premium but warm, existentially dramatic**. We acknowledge what we're making‚Äîa gacha game designed to captivate, monetize, and entertain. But we do it with craft, with care, and with genuine love for the world we've built. Our aesthetic is warm and welcoming; our stakes are real.

This document defines how Infinite Idol speaks across all channels, from Twitter threads to Discord announcements, from Ika's in-character posts to behind-the-scenes founder updates.

## Core Voice Pillars

### 1. Shameless But Genuine

**What It Means**

We don't hide from what we are. We're making a gacha game. We want players to spend money. We design systems that encourage engagement and spending. This is not something to apologize for‚Äîit's something to own with confidence.

But here's the twist: we're also genuine. We actually care about the world, the characters, the story. This isn't cynical IP extraction. We love Ika. We believe in the Eternal Stage. We want players to feel something real.

**How It Sounds**

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| "Yes, it's a gacha game. A really good one." | "It's not *really* about the gacha..." |
| "We designed this banner to be irresistible." | "We hope you'll consider supporting us." |
| "Ika wants your Devotion. So do we." | "Please become a fan if you can!" |
| "We built this world because we love it." | "This is just a business venture." |

**Examples**

> "New banner dropping tomorrow. We put our heart into designing these cosmetics to separate you from your SUI. And honestly? They're gorgeous. You're going to want them."

> "We're a gacha game. We're Web3. We're anime idols. Everything you've been warned about. But we're also genuinely good. Stick around and we'll prove it."

---

### 2. Self-Aware

**What It Means**

We know the tropes. We know the criticisms. We know what people say about gacha games, about Web3 games, about idol games. Instead of pretending we're different, we acknowledge the landscape and then transcend it.

Self-awareness isn't self-deprecation. We're not mocking ourselves‚Äîwe're demonstrating intelligence. We see the meta-game and we play it knowingly.

**How It Sounds**

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| "Another gacha game? Yes. But hear us out." | "We're totally unique and different!" |
| "You've seen this mechanic before. We do it better." | "We invented revolutionary gameplay." |
| "Web3 gaming has a reputation. We're here to change it." | "Web3 gaming is perfect and misunderstood!" |
| "Idols chasing Senpai. It's exactly what you think. And more." | "Our narrative is incomparably original." |

**Examples**

> "Yes, the odds are 3% for Legendary. Yes, you're going to pull anyway. Yes, we know. That's why we made the Legendary items this good."

> "Infinite Idol is built on SUI. Before you roll your eyes‚Äîwe're gameplay-first, blockchain-second. The crypto part exists to make ownership real, not to make us look innovative."

---

### 3. Premium But Warm

**What It Means**

We are polished. We are inviting. We are pink and warm gold, celebratory and vibrant. We're in the Blue Archive / Uma Musume tier‚Äîpremium quality with warmth and accessibility.

Our aesthetic is:
- **Pink**: Ika's signature, warm and inviting
- **Warm Gold**: Sunset tones, celebrations, success
- **Soft Lighting**: Welcoming, not harsh
- **Polished Execution**: Every element feels crafted and intentional

**How It Sounds**

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| "Premium cosmetics for idols who shine." | "Cheap knockoffs that nobody wants." |
| "The Eternal Stage awaits." | "Just another boring stage." |
| "Celebrate with your favorites!" | "Generic reward notification." |
| "Stakes are real. Style is too." | "Everything is super serious and dark." |

**Language Guidelines**

- Use **confident vocabulary**: "shine" not "try", "celebrate" not "participate"
- Cute is allowed: warmth and charm are on-brand
- Embrace dramatic phrasing when stakes are high: "The Eternal Stage demands" > "The Stage wants"
- Keep sentences confident: declarative statements, not questions seeking validation
- Balance warmth with weight: the story is dark, but delivery can be warm

**Examples**

> "The new Sakura Festival collection. Five pieces. Pink and gold. For idols who shine brightest in bloom."

> "Ika's latest outfit captures everything she is‚Äîdetermined, vibrant, and impossible to ignore."

---

### 4. Existentially Dramatic

**What It Means**

In Infinite Idol, the stakes are real. Idols don't just lose‚Äîthey Fade from existence. Fans don't just watch‚Äîthey literally keep their idols alive. This existential weight informs everything.

We lean into the drama. Every Chase matters. Every ranking shift is life or death. Every fan gained is survival. This isn't overdramatic‚Äîwithin our world, it's simply accurate.

**How It Sounds**

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| "Your Devotion keeps her existing." | "Support your favorite idol!" |
| "Fight for your place on the Eternal Stage." | "Compete in fun events!" |
| "47 fans. It's not enough. It's barely existing." | "She needs more fans to be popular!" |
| "The Foundation controls who lives and who Fades." | "The Foundation manages the rankings." |

**Tone Calibration**

- **Heavy moments**: Full existential weight, no humor
- **Lighter moments**: Dramatic undercurrent, but room for wit
- **Hype moments**: Channel existential stakes into excitement
- **Community moments**: Warmth exists within the darkness

**Examples**

> "Tomorrow's Chase determines who rises and who fades. There is no participation trophy. Only survival."

> "Ika woke up with 47 fans. Most idols have thousands. She exists on borrowed time and borrowed devotion. And she wouldn't have it any other way."

> "Every gem you spend fuels the Eternal Stage. Every idol you support delays their Fading. This isn't a game. It's a lifeline."

## Content Type Guidelines

### Twitter/X

**Single Tweets**
- Max 240 characters for optimal readability
- Hook in first 5 words
- End with call-to-action or engagement prompt
- Hashtags: #InfiniteIdol #SUI (always), topical tags as relevant

**Threads**
- 5-10 tweets per thread
- Hook in first tweet, CTA in last
- Each tweet stands alone but builds on previous
- Visual breaks every 2-3 tweets

**Tone Distribution**
- Ika Voice: Playful, direct, intimate
- Lore Drops: Mysterious, dramatic, tantalizing
- Founder Hype: Confident, ambitious, community-focused
- Engagement: Warm, appreciative, inclusive

### Discord

**Announcements**
- Clear, structured formatting
- Hype without desperation
- Action items clearly stated
- Exclusive/special treatment emphasized

**Community Events**
- Dramatic framing even for casual activities
- Seven Gates hierarchy acknowledged
- Rewards clearly communicated
- Lore integration where possible

**Moderation**
- Firm but fair
- Never break character without clear markers
- Direct issues privately, celebrate publicly

### Long-Form Content

**Articles/Blog Posts**
- Deep lore exploration allowed
- Maintain voice even in technical content
- Visual breaks every 200-300 words
- Conclude with emotional resonance

**Patch Notes/Updates**
- Lead with most exciting change
- Technical accuracy required
- Hype framing: "unleashed" > "released"
- Thank community genuinely

All content is rated on a 0-4 scale:

| Level | Name | Description | Verdict |

| **DS-0** | Normie | Corporate marketing speak. No personality, no edge. Could come from any brand. | ‚ùå **REJECT** - Start over |

| **DS-3** | Degen | Peak authenticity. Self-aware, shameless, culturally sharp. Makes the audience feel "in on it." | üî• **OPTIMAL** - Target zone |
| **DS-4** | Too Far | Crossed from edgy to offensive, or so niche it alienates. Brand safety risk. | ‚ö†Ô∏è **PULL BACK** - Dial it down |

### Cultural Authenticity Markers

Content should demonstrate fluency in:

**Crypto Culture**
- CT vocabulary (alpha, ape, fud, ngmi, wagmi, degen, based, diamond hands)
- Memecoin awareness without specific shilling
- Web3 native sensibilities (ownership, community, decentralization vibes)
- SUI ecosystem positioning

**Gacha Culture**
- Pull culture (salt, copium, "got them in one", skill issue)
- Whale/F2P dynamics awareness
- Banner psychology (bait, limited, must-pull)
- Pity and spark systems

**Otaku Culture**
- Waifu/husbando vocabulary
- Seasonal anime awareness
- Trope fluency (dere types, isekai, etc.)
- Fan service norms and boundaries
- Idol industry meta

- [ ] Does it embody "shameless but genuine"?

- [ ] Does it maintain our premium but warm aesthetic?
- [ ] Are stakes appropriately existential?
- [ ] Would Ika approve of the tone?

- [ ] Does it respect the world we've built?
- [ ] Is it welcoming, not pleading?

- [ ] Is the DS calibrated for the target platform?
- [ ] Does it demonstrate cultural fluency (crypto/gacha/otaku)?
- [ ] Are there any DS-0 red flags to eliminate?
